Color Scheme Explanation — Visual Diagram
Complementary Color Scheme Diagram
✓ Used Here
Complementary
Two colors directly opposite on the wheel — maximum contrast.
Blue + Amber are 180° apart. Cool blue creates depth and professionalism; warm amber creates instant focus. This contrast is why CTAs on blue backgrounds demand attention.
Monochromatic
Single hue in multiple shades — elegant but low energy.
All blues from near-black to pale. Extremely cohesive and refined but provides no natural focal point for CTAs. Less visual hierarchy than complementary.
Triadic
Three hues equally spaced 120° apart — vibrant but complex.
Blue + Green + Purple. Creates a lively palette but harder to keep professional. Complementary scheme provides cleaner hierarchy for a portfolio.
Analogous
Adjacent colors on the wheel — harmonious, low contrast.
Blue + Teal + Indigo all sit next to each other. Beautiful harmony for wellness or nature brands, but lacks the punch of a complementary accent for tech portfolios.

Section 07
Color Psychology — Click each card to explore

Colors carry emotional meaning. Understanding this helps designers create intentional emotional responses.

+
Blue
"Trust the process"
Blue is the world's most universally liked color. It conveys trust, reliability, intelligence, and calm. Tech and finance brands use blue to signal competence and security.
Used by:
FacebookTwitterPayPalSamsungIBM
+
Amber
"Act now"
Amber and orange signal urgency, energy, and warmth. They create a sense of action and optimism. Used for CTAs because the eye naturally jumps to warm hues in cool surroundings.
Used by:
AmazonHarley-DavidsonFantaIMDB
+
Indigo
"Thoughtful + Creative"
Indigo and violet sit between blue's calm and red's energy. They evoke creativity, mystery, and wisdom. Perfect for bridging warm and cool elements in a design system.
Used by:
TwitchFigmaNotionMonzo
+
Green
"Growth + Safety"
Green is universally associated with nature, growth, health, and go. In UI, green signals success states (✓ check marks), progress, and eco-friendly brands.
Used by:
WhatsAppSpotifyWhole FoodsRobinhood
+
Red
"Danger + Passion"
Red raises heart rate and creates urgency. It means danger (errors, alerts) but also passion and appetite (food brands). Use sparingly — it competes aggressively for attention.
Used by:
YouTubeCoca-ColaNetflixPinterest

Normal Vision
Full color perception — all three cone types (red, green, blue) function normally. Approximately 92% of men and 99.5% of women see colors this way.
Affects ~0% of population
Color-Only vs. Inclusive Design

When only color communicates meaning, color blind users lose information. Adding icons and labels makes it universally readable.
